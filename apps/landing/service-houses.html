Constructing What Others Can Stand On

Leadership, contribution, and legacy

This house structure is for schools developing students who take initiative, serve their communities, and build things that outlast them.

About This House Structure

Champions of Service is designed for schools that emphasize leadership, service, and contribution over competition.

Each House represents a different dimension of building — courage to begin, selflessness in service, initiative in exploration, and endurance through difficulty.

Students don't earn their House — they grow within it. The House becomes a lens for understanding how to develop that particular strength over time.

Guiding Question

What do students build that others can rely on?

The Four Houses

Training Grounds for Leadership

Each House represents a different dimension of building something lasting.

House of Salmān al-Fārisī

Preparing the way with courage and purpose (Musābaqah)

Failure It Addresses

Waiting for others to lead, avoiding responsibility, or expecting success without stepping forward first.

Looks Like

  • Stepping forward when others hesitate
  • Taking initiative to create positive change
  • Building strong foundations others can rely on
  • Giving time, effort, and comfort for something greater

House of Abū Ayyūb al-Anṣārī

Service that builds strong communities (Khidmah)

Failure It Addresses

Only helping when it's easy, visible, or personally beneficial.

Looks Like

  • Helping without being asked
  • Putting group needs before personal comfort
  • Supporting classmates, teachers, and systems
  • Being dependable and present

House of Nusaybah bint Kaʿb

Courage that protects values and people (Shajāʿah)

Failure It Addresses

Staying silent when something wrong is happening or backing away under pressure.

Looks Like

  • Standing up for what is right
  • Protecting others from harm or injustice
  • Speaking and acting when it's uncomfortable
  • Showing bravery through action

House of Asmāʾ bint Abī Bakr

Reliability and endurance under pressure (Thabāt)

Failure It Addresses

Giving up, folding under pressure, or dropping responsibility when things get hard.

Looks Like

  • Staying committed even when no one is watching
  • Carrying responsibility without complaining
  • Remaining calm and steady during difficulty
  • Being trustworthy in stressful situations
